Garbodor (XY competitive strategy):
Garbodor stands out as the most consistent setter of Spikes in the NU tier, a feat attributed to its substantial bulk and strategic typing, which enable it to reliably deploy entry hazards throughout a battle. Its pure Poison typing grants it advantageous resistances to Bug, Poison, Grass, and Fighting, positioning it as a capable counter to common threats like Scyther, Skuntank, Hariyama, Hitmonchan, Lilligant, and Primeape. Uniquely among the tier's defensive Pokémon, Garbodor possesses the capability to defeat standard forms of Mega Audino. Thanks to its inherent, well-rounded bulk, Garbodor readily endures successive attacks while gradually diminishing opponents' health through Rocky Helmet's passive damage. Furthermore, it boasts a potent ability, Aftermath, which can severely weaken or even incapacitate physical attackers such as Tauros, Kangaskhan, Zangoose, Samurott, Klinklang, and Liepard, should they be the ones to knock Garbodor out with a contact move.

Garbodor also commands a diverse offensive movepool, featuring options like Focus Blast, Thunderbolt, Seed Bomb, and Psychic, which can be leveraged to surprise various checks, including Steelix, Aggron, Pelipper, Rhydon, Piloswine, Weezing, and Gastrodon. Regrettably, its typing also exposes it to common Ground-type coverage and a specific weakness to Psychic-type attacks. A notable absence of reliable recovery mechanisms means Garbodor is prone to quick wear-down, often compromising its effectiveness in deterring physical attackers.

Spikes rapidly depletes the HP of grounded Pokémon through residual damage. Toxic Spikes, meanwhile, accelerates the attrition of bulky, defensively oriented teams, as these compositions frequently possess recovery methods that can otherwise mitigate the impact of Spikes alone. Gunk Shot delivers considerable damage to foes that do not resist it, benefits from STAB, and offers a chance to poison Xatu, thereby frustrating its repeated attempts to switch in and reflect Garbodor's meticulously placed entry hazards. Seed Bomb offers targeted coverage against prevalent Pokémon resistant to Gunk Shot, such as Gastrodon, Claydol, Rhydon, Omastar, and Kabutops. Drain Punch presents an optional move that can afford Garbodor some passive HP restoration while providing a means to strike Steel-types like Klinklang, Ferroseed, Aggron, and Steelix; however, its power output is rather limited, especially against the latter. Focus Blast serves as an alternative, enabling Garbodor to inflict significantly greater damage on Steelix and Aggron while retaining coverage against Rock-, Steel-, and Ice-types by exploiting their often lower Special Defense stat. Rock Blast is another viable option for more easily managing Scyther, and it reliably damages Xatu and other Flying-types upon their switch-in.

Allocating 56 Speed EVs allows Garbodor to outspeed maximum Speed Mega Audino, Aggron, and Jolly Rhydon. Alternatively, 96 Speed EVs can be employed to outpace neutral-natured Pokémon with full investment in their base 55 Speed stat, such as Cacturne, Exeggutor, and Omastar. Maximizing HP EVs enhances Garbodor's total bulk. The remaining EVs are channeled into Defense, coupled with an Impish nature, to uphold Garbodor's substantial physical bulk and penalize Pokémon employing physical contact moves, for example, Samurott, Scyther, and Hitmonchan.

Aftermath assists Garbodor in its role as a counter to physical attackers dependent on contact-based offenses. Rocky Helmet consistently applies chip damage, serving to deter Rapid Spin users from clearing hazards unless they are prepared to endure significant recoil, and generally wears down physical attackers. Black Sludge is an alternative item choice, providing gradual HP recovery to extend Garbodor's staying power.

Garbodor's choice of entry hazard should be dictated by the composition of the opposing team. Against teams that lean on bulk for countering Pokémon, Spikes offers rapid attrition. Conversely, teams lacking status removal or a Poison-type Pokémon will struggle significantly against Toxic Spikes, which inflicts steady passive damage upon their Pokémon. Drain Punch should only be deployed against fragile threats like Liepard, given that robust Rock- and Steel-types such as Steelix, Regirock, and Rhydon can readily absorb the hit, making the deployment of Spikes or Toxic Spikes layers a more advantageous play. Likewise, it is generally more productive for Garbodor to establish Spikes rather than employing Seed Bomb against physically bulky targets like Aurorus and Regirock.

Garbodor is capable of switching into contact moves such as Kangaskhan's Fake Out, Hariyama's Bullet Punch, and Hitmonchan's Drain Punch to accumulate Rocky Helmet damage. Nonetheless, caution is advised, as numerous physical attackers often carry Earthquake for coverage against Garbodor, simultaneously circumventing Rocky Helmet and Aftermath's recoil effects. Gunk Shot should be employed against anticipated switches to entry hazard removers like Xatu, Pelipper, and Mantine, being Garbodor's most potent attack in such scenarios. Ultimately, if Garbodor's health is depleted and its primary duties are fulfilled, it can be strategically sacrificed to a Pokémon utilizing a contact move – for instance, a weakened Rapid Spin user – to inflict substantial recoil damage via Rocky Helmet and Aftermath, thereby preventing the removal of entry hazards if the spinner is KO'd.

Steelix emerges as an excellent companion, forming a comprehensive entry hazard core where Steelix neutralizes problematic Psychic-types like Mesprit and Musharna, and Garbodor, in turn, absorbs Fighting-type assaults directed at Steelix. Ghost-type Pokémon make effective allies by providing spinblocking capabilities. Rotom offers momentum with Volt Switch, and Mismagius can thwart Defog attempts via Taunt; both are also capable of intercepting Ground-type attacks intended for Garbodor. Dark-types, exemplified by Liepard, are well-suited to confront Ghost- and Psychic-types that resist Garbodor's offensive moves. Liepard is especially valuable due to its capacity to Pursuit trap Xatu, facilitating Garbodor's hazard deployment. Furthermore, Liepard benefits from a resilient Pokémon resistant to Fighting-type attacks to pivot into. Water-types like Samurott, Floatzel, and Poliwrath excel at dismantling the Rock- and Ground-types that impede Garbodor, while Garbodor itself can manage certain Grass-types such as Lilligant, Shiftry, and Cacturne. Psychic-types such as Musharna, Mesprit, and Xatu can compel opposing Poison-types to switch out.

Spikes inflicts attrition on grounded Pokémon, which is advantageous given Garbodor's ability to compel frequent switches. With Attack investment, Gunk Shot strikes exceptionally hard, consistently achieving a 2HKO against Pokémon including bulky Xatu, Hitmonchan, Mantine, and Mega Audino. Drain Punch enables Garbodor to engage certain Steel-types, such as Klinklang and Pawniard, while also recovering a fair portion of its HP. Nevertheless, the less accurate Focus Blast connects with Steelix, Ferroseed, Aggron, and Regirock with considerably greater force. Invested Seed Bomb can reliably secure 2HKOs against Gastrodon and Rhydon, and substantially damage Claydol, which otherwise might force Garbodor's retreat with its Ground-type STAB attacks. Explosion proves valuable for preventing Rapid Spin and Defog, and it can critically damage spinners such as Claydol. Toxic Spikes additionally serves as an effective choice for pressuring more defensively inclined teams.

With maximum Speed EVs, Garbodor can outpace neutral-natured Pokémon having a base 70 Speed stat, such as Samurott and Ludicolo, and Hasty Abomasnow. A Jolly nature allows Garbodor to outspeed defensive Xatu, proving highly beneficial as it can secure a 2HKO with Gunk Shot before deploying hazards. Furthermore, this nature enables Garbodor to outspeed Magmortar, Modest Mesprit, Adamant Kabutops, and Lonely Shiftry. Alternatively, an Adamant nature amplifies Garbodor's offensive might, empowering it to strike Pokémon such as Xatu and Rhydon with immense force.

Aftermath supports Garbodor in its role as a counter to physical attackers like Klinklang and Hariyama. Rocky Helmet delivers chip damage to penalize physical attackers. Life Orb provides an option for a substantial boost in damage output, allowing Garbodor to compel numerous switches. Poison Barb offers an alternative, avoiding Life Orb's recoil while still enabling formidable damage with Gunk Shot. As another option, Garbodor can deploy a specially offensive moveset comprising Gunk Shot, Giga Drain, Focus Blast, Thunderbolt, and Psychic to bait out particular checks and dispatch them using its extensive coverage. With Attack investment, Garbodor gains greater flexibility in its move choices, enabling it to surprise frequent counters like Rhydon and Gastrodon. Offensive investment allows Garbodor to more consistently lure and defeat common hazard removers or blockers, including Xatu, Mantine, and Pelipper. Max Speed EVs (252) permit Garbodor to outspeed a wider array of Pokémon, enhancing its capacity to deploy Spikes against more opponents. This configuration functions as a valuable pivot for offense-focused teams, offering substantial inherent bulk, a favorable defensive typing, and the offensive punch to threaten problematic Rapid Spin or Defog users, especially Xatu. While common physical attackers like Kangaskhan and Hariyama might employ Earthquake to circumvent Aftermath and Rocky Helmet chip damage, an offensively oriented Garbodor is capable of 2HKOing both. Should Garbodor's HP be low after completing its tasks, this set retains utility as an emergency response to potent physical attackers, dealing approximately 41% of the foe's HP via combined Rocky Helmet and Aftermath damage. Garbodor ought to use Explosion against Pokémon such as Pelipper, Mantine, Prinplup, and Skuntank to obstruct their Defog attempts. This particular set finds greater utility on bulky offense teams, enabling it to maintain offensive pressure while also efficiently establishing entry hazards. Pokémon such as Kangaskhan, Floatzel, Tauros, and Archeops benefit from their counters being progressively weakened, allowing them to sustain offensive pressure. Users of Volt Switch and U-turn, including Scyther, Mesprit, and Manectric, gain significant advantage from the entry hazards set by Garbodor.
