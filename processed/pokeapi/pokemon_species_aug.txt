Title: Rewritten Pokémon Reference

Bulbasaur, classified as a Seed Pokémon, possesses the dual types of Grass/Poison. Its foundational attributes include 45 HP, an Attack of 49, a Defense stat of 49, Special Attack and Special Defense both at 65, and a Speed of 45. Its primary ability is Overgrow, with Chlorophyll serving as its hidden ability. This creature belongs to the Monster and Plant Egg groups, thrives in Grassland habitats, and was introduced in Generation I. It exhibits a Medium Slow growth rate and evolves into Ivysaur at level 16, which then evolves into Venusaur at level 32. From the moment of its birth, a peculiar seed is ingrained on its back, establishing a symbiotic relationship where the developing plant grows alongside the Pokémon. This creature can sustain itself for prolonged periods without consuming any food, as the seed on its back serves as a repository for both energy and essential nutrients. The seed gradually increases in size, mirroring the Pokémon's own growth. During its formative stages, Bulbasaur leverages these stored nutrients for its development. It is often observed resting in direct sunlight, allowing the seed on its back to progressively expand by absorbing the sun's powerful rays.

Ivysaur, also a Seed Pokémon, shares the Grass/Poison typing. Its base statistics are 60 HP, 62 Attack, 63 Defense, 80 Special Attack, 80 Special Defense, and 60 Speed. Its standard ability is Overgrow, with Chlorophyll as its hidden ability. Like its pre-evolution, it belongs to the Monster and Plant Egg groups, resides in Grassland areas, and originates from Generation I, maintaining a Medium Slow growth trajectory. It evolves from Bulbasaur at level 16 and proceeds to Venusaur at level 32. As the bulb on its back expands, the Pokémon seemingly struggles to stand on its rear legs. This burgeoning bulb thrives by absorbing both energy and nutrients. A sweet and pleasant aroma emanates from the bulb when it is nearing its full bloom, signaling that a large flower is soon to blossom. Increased exposure to sunlight not only enhances Ivysaur's inherent strength but also significantly contributes to the growth of the bud. An observable increase in time spent basking in sunlight is a clear indicator that the bud is on the verge of transforming into a prominent flower. To counteract the increased mass of its evolving plant, Ivysaur's legs and trunk develop increased thickness and strength, providing crucial support.

Venusaur, the final evolution in its line, is designated as a Seed Pokémon, retaining the Grass/Poison typing. Its formidable base stats are 80 HP, 82 Attack, 83 Defense, 100 Special Attack, 100 Special Defense, and 80 Speed. Its primary ability is Overgrow, with Chlorophyll as its hidden ability. This Pokémon is part of the Monster and Plant Egg groups, preferring Grassland environments, and was introduced in Generation I. It maintains a Medium Slow growth rate, evolving from Ivysaur at level 32, which itself evolved from Bulbasaur at level 16. A substantial flower adorns Venusaur's back, which fully blossoms when the Pokémon is actively absorbing solar energy. It perpetually moves to locate and gather sunlight, extending its wide petals to capture the sun's rays, thereby converting them into vital energy to empower its body. This conversion capability renders it especially potent during the summer season. The flower's petals emit a delightful fragrance as Venusaur basks and absorbs sunlight. This captivating aroma, which intensifies after a rainy day, is known to attract other Pokémon and possess a soothing effect on human emotions, even calming those involved in combat. The flower itself is reputed to display vibrant hues when nourished by ample sunlight and nutrition.

Charmander is classified as a Lizard Pokémon, featuring the Fire type. Its foundational stats are 39 HP, 52 Attack, 43 Defense, 60 Special Attack, 50 Special Defense, and 65 Speed. Its standard ability is Blaze, with Solar Power as its hidden ability. This Pokémon belongs to the Monster and Dragon Egg groups, inhabits Mountain regions, and hails from Generation I. It experiences a Medium Slow growth rate, evolving into Charmeleon at level 16, which then evolves into Charizard at level 36. A flame ignited at the very tip of Charmander's tail from its birth, and its life is inextricably linked to this fire; should it extinguish, the Pokémon would perish. This burning flame serves as a direct gauge of its life force: a strong, vibrant blaze signifies robust health, while a faint flicker indicates weakness. A healthy flame will burn vigorously, even if slightly dampened. The tail flame also mirrors Charmander's emotional state, gently wavering when content or happy, but flaring fiercely when enraged. This Pokémon demonstrates a clear affinity for warm environments. When it rains, vapor is reportedly emitted from its tail-tip. In tranquil settings, a subtle sound can be discerned from its burning tail.

Charmeleon, known as a Flame Pokémon, is exclusively Fire-typed. Its base stats are 58 HP, 64 Attack, 58 Defense, 80 Special Attack, 65 Special Defense, and 80 Speed. Blaze is its standard ability, complemented by Solar Power as its hidden ability. It shares the Monster and Dragon Egg groups, is found in Mountain habitats, and debuted in Generation I. It maintains a Medium Slow growth rate, evolving from Charmander at level 16 and subsequently into Charizard at level 36. Charmeleon possesses an inherently fiery and hot-headed disposition, perpetually seeking out adversaries and only finding tranquility upon achieving victory. Its barbaric nature is evident in combat, where it ruthlessly employs sharp claws to devastate foes. When engaging in battle, it whirls its scorching tail, drastically elevating the ambient temperature to unbearable levels. If sufficiently provoked or agitated during a strenuous fight, it unleashes potent flames, capable of incinerating its immediate vicinity. In an intensified state of excitement, particularly when facing formidable opponents, the flame on its tail flares with a distinctive bluish-white hue, and it may discharge similar flames.
