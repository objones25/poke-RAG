Title: Pokémon Ball Descriptions

The Master Ball functions as a Pokémon item. When deployed in combat, it unfailingly captures any wild Pokémon. Should it be utilized during a trainer battle, it will have no effect, and the item will be expended. This is renowned as the premier Poké Ball, boasting unparalleled performance, guaranteed to secure any wild Pokémon.

The Ultra Ball is a Pokémon item. Its use in battle involves an attempt to capture a wild Pokémon, applying a 2× catch rate modifier. Activating it in a trainer battle yields no result and consumes the item. This superior Poké Ball offers a significantly increased success rate for capturing Pokémon compared to a Great Ball.

The Great Ball functions as a Pokémon item. When deployed in combat, it endeavors to catch a wild Pokémon, utilizing a 1.5× catch rate. In a trainer encounter, its activation is fruitless, and the ball is forfeited. This is a reliable, high-performance Poké Ball, offering an improved success rate for capturing Pokémon over a standard Poké Ball.

The Poké Ball is a standard Pokémon item. When used during a battle, it attempts to capture a wild Pokémon with a 1× catch rate. Its application in a trainer battle results in no action, and the item is consumed. This fundamental device for catching wild Pokémon is designed as a capsule system, thrown to comfortably encapsulate its target.

The Safari Ball serves as a specialized Pokémon item. In combat, it tries to capture a wild Pokémon with a 1.5× catch rate. Its utility is strictly confined to specific locations: the Great Marsh or the Kanto Safari Zone. This unique Poké Ball is distinguished by its camouflage pattern, reflecting its use in these particular environments.

The Net Ball is a Pokémon item for capturing wild Pokémon. When employed in battle, its catch rate is 3× if the target is a Water-type or Bug-type Pokémon; otherwise, it operates at a 1× catch rate. As with others, using it against a trainer's Pokémon has no effect and results in its loss. This distinctive Poké Ball is notably more effective against Water- or Bug-type creatures.

The Dive Ball is a Pokémon item primarily used for capturing wild Pokémon. In a battle, its catch rate is elevated to 3.5× if the wild Pokémon was encountered through surfing or fishing; otherwise, it defaults to a 1× rate. Attempting to use it in a trainer battle will be futile, and the item will be consumed. This particular Poké Ball proves especially proficient when attempting to catch Pokémon found underwater.

The Nest Ball functions as a Pokémon item for wild Pokémon capture attempts. In battle, its catch rate is dynamically determined by the formula `(40 - level) / 10`, where `level` represents the wild Pokémon’s level, peaking at 3.9× for a level 1 Pokémon. For Pokémon with a level exceeding 30, the catch rate is 1×. Deploying it in a trainer battle has no effect, and the ball is lost. This distinctive Poké Ball gains effectiveness as the wild Pokémon's level decreases.

The Repeat Ball is a Pokémon item designed for capturing wild Pokémon. During a battle, if the wild Pokémon's species is already recorded as caught in the trainer’s Pokédex, it provides a 3× catch rate; otherwise, the rate is 1×. Activating it in a trainer battle is ineffective, leading to its loss. This particular Poké Ball is especially efficient at re-catching Pokémon species previously encountered and captured.

The Timer Ball functions as a Pokémon item for capturing wild Pokémon. Its catch rate in battle begins at 1.1× on the first turn and progressively rises by 0.1× with each subsequent turn, capping at 4× by turn 30. If deployed in a trainer battle, it has no effect and is consumed. This distinctive Poké Ball demonstrates increasing effectiveness the longer a battle persists.

The Luxury Ball is a Pokémon item used for attempting to catch a wild Pokémon, employing a 1× catch rate. A unique property of this ball is that any Pokémon caught within it experiences an additional point increase to its happiness whenever its happiness would normally rise. As expected, usage in a trainer battle is ineffective and results in the item's disposal. It is known for making captured Pokémon quickly grow more amiable.

The Premier Ball is a Pokémon item. It is utilized in battle to attempt the capture of a wild Pokémon, functioning at a 1× catch rate. Its deployment against a trainer's Pokémon will yield no result, and the ball will be expended. This somewhat rare Poké Ball is uniquely crafted to memorialize a special event.

The Dusk Ball is a Pokémon item for capturing wild Pokémon. When activated in battle, it offers a heightened 3.5× catch rate if it is currently nighttime or if the wild Pokémon was encountered within a cave; otherwise, its rate is 1×. Using it in a trainer battle is ineffective, and the ball is lost. This specialized Poké Ball is engineered to enhance the success of catching Pokémon during the night or in subterranean environments.

The Heal Ball is a Pokémon item. Its function in battle is to attempt to capture a wild Pokémon with a 1× catch rate. Upon a successful capture, the newly caught Pokémon immediately has its HP fully restored, the PP for all its moves replenished, and any existing status ailments cured. If used in a trainer battle, nothing occurs, and the ball is consumed.
